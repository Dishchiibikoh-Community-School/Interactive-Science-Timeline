Interactive Science Timeline

A Journey Through Scientific Discovery

Prehistoric Era (before 1500 CE)

~2.5 million years ago

First Stone Tools

Early humans (Homo habilis) created simple stone tools for cutting and hunting.

Significance: Marked the beginning of technology and gave humans an evolutionary advantage.

~1 million years ago

Control of Fire

Evidence shows early humans learned to make and control fire.

Significance: Provided warmth, protection, cooking, and social gathering, shaping human evolution.

~10,000 BCE

Development of Agriculture

Humans began farming in the Fertile Crescent and other regions.

Significance: Led to permanent settlements, population growth, and the rise of civilizations.

Ancient Civilizations

Early Astronomy

Egyptians, Babylonians, and Mayans tracked stars and planets to create calendars.

Significance: Allowed for accurate farming cycles, navigation, and early scientific thought.

1500s - The Dawn of Modern Science

1543

Copernicus & Heliocentrism

Publishes "On the Revolutions of the Celestial Spheres," proposing the Sun-centered model of the universe.

Significance: Challenged geocentric belief and reshaped astronomy.

1545

Andreas Vesalius & Human Anatomy

Publishes "De Humani Corporis Fabrica," the first detailed and accurate study of human anatomy.

Significance: Corrected ancient misconceptions and advanced medicine and surgery.

1572

Tycho Brahe & the Supernova

Proved the heavens were not unchanging, contradicting Aristotelian views.

Significance: Paved the way for modern astronomy and understanding of stellar life cycles.

1590s

Invention of the Compound Microscope

Attributed to Hans & Zacharias Janssen.

Significance: Allowed scientists to study microorganisms and cells for the first time.

1600s - Scientific Revolution

1609

Galileo Improves the Telescope

Observed Jupiter's moons, lunar craters, and phases of Venus.

Significance: Provided strong evidence for heliocentrism.

1620

Francis Bacon & Scientific Method

Publishes "Novum Organum," introducing the scientific method.

Significance: Laid the foundation for modern scientific inquiry.

1665

Robert Hooke Discovers Cells

Publishes "Micrographia," coining the term "cell".

Significance: Birth of cell theory in biology.

1687

Isaac Newton's Principia Mathematica

Defined laws of motion and universal gravitation.

Significance: Revolutionized physics and astronomy.

1700s - Enlightenment & Discovery

1735

Carl Linnaeus & Systema Naturae

Introduced binomial nomenclature, the system for naming species.

Significance: Foundation of modern biological classification.

1752

Benjamin Franklin's Kite Experiment

Demonstrated that lightning is electricity.

Significance: Led to the invention of the lightning rod and advanced electrical science.

1774

Joseph Priestley Discovers Oxygen

Identified a gas that supported combustion and respiration.

Significance: Fundamental to chemistry and medicine.

1785

James Hutton & Uniformitarianism

Proposed that Earth's features formed over long periods through gradual processes.

Significance: Laid groundwork for modern geology.

1800s - Age of Invention & Modern Biology

1800

Alessandro Volta Invents the Battery

First true source of continuous electrical current.

Significance: Enabled experiments that fueled the study of electricity and chemistry.

1827

Georg Ohm Formulates Ohm's Law

Relationship between voltage, current, and resistance.

Significance: Fundamental principle for electrical circuits and modern electronics.

1859

Charles Darwin's "On the Origin of Species"

Introduced the theory of evolution by natural selection.

Significance: Revolutionized biology, medicine, and our understanding of life.

1869

Dmitri Mendeleev Creates the Periodic Table

Organized chemical elements by atomic weight and properties.

Significance: Provided a framework for modern chemistry and predicted new elements.

1900s - Breakthroughs & Space Age

1905

Albert Einstein's "Annus Mirabilis"

Introduced special relativity and groundbreaking physics theories.

Significance: Changed our understanding of time, space, and energy.

1928

Alexander Fleming Discovers Penicillin

First true antibiotic, revolutionizing medicine and saving millions of lives.

1953

Watson & Crick Describe DNA Structure

Discovery of the double helix with key contributions from Rosalind Franklin.

Significance: Foundation of molecular biology and genetics.

1969

Apollo 11 Moon Landing

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon.

Significance: Major milestones in space exploration and human achievement.

2000s - 21st Century Science

2001

Human Genome Project Draft

First map of all human genes.

Significance: Revolutionized medicine, genetics, and biotechnology.

2007

Launch of the iPhone

Combined phone, music player, and internet into one device.

Significance: Transformed communication, computing, and global culture.

2012

Discovery of the Higgs Boson

Confirmed the existence of the Higgs field, essential for understanding mass.

Significance: Major victory for particle physics and the Standard Model.

2019/2020

First Image of a Black Hole

Captured by the Event Horizon Telescope (M87 Galaxy).

Significance: Historic proof of Einstein's theory of general relativity on a cosmic scale.
