Historical Impact & Significance

Prehistoric Era (before 1500 CE)
~2.5 million years ago
First Stone Tools
Early humans (Homo habilis) created simple stone tools for cutting and hunting.
Significance: Marked the beginning of technology and gave humans an evolutionary advantage.
~1 million years ago
Control of Fire
Evidence shows early humans learned to make and control fire.
Significance: Provided warmth, protection, cooking, and social gathering, shaping human evolution.
~10,000 BCE
Development of Agriculture
Humans began farming in the Fertile Crescent and other regions.
Significance: Led to permanent settlements, population growth, and the rise of civilizations.
Ancient Civilizations
Early Astronomy
Egyptians, Babylonians, and Mayans tracked stars and planets to create calendars.
Significance: Allowed for accurate farming cycles, navigation, and early scientific thought.
1500s ‚Äì The Dawn of Modern Science
1543
Copernicus Publishes On the Revolutions of the Celestial Spheres
Proposed the heliocentric model, placing the Sun at the center of the universe instead of Earth.
Significance: Challenged centuries of geocentric belief and reshaped astronomy.
1545
Andreas Vesalius Publishes De Humani Corporis Fabrica
First detailed and accurate study of human anatomy.
Significance: Corrected ancient misconceptions and advanced medicine and surgery.
1609
Galileo Improves the Telescope
Observed Jupiter's moons, lunar craters, and phases of Venus.
Significance: Provided strong evidence for heliocentrism.
1600s ‚Äì Scientific Revolution
1687
Isaac Newton Publishes Principia Mathematica
Defined laws of motion and universal gravitation.
Significance: Revolutionized physics and astronomy.
1665
Robert Hooke Publishes Micrographia
Coined the term "cell" while observing cork under a microscope.
Significance: Birth of cell theory in biology.
1800s ‚Äì Age of Invention & Modern Biology
1859
Charles Darwin Publishes On the Origin of Species
Introduced the theory of evolution by natural selection.
Significance: Revolutionized biology, medicine, and our understanding of life.
1869
Dmitri Mendeleev Creates the Periodic Table
Organized chemical elements by atomic weight and properties.
Significance: Provided a framework for modern chemistry and predicted new elements.
1900s ‚Äì Scientific Breakthroughs & Space Age
1905
Albert Einstein's Annus Mirabilis Papers
Introduced special relativity and groundbreaking physics theories.
Significance: Changed our understanding of time, space, and energy.
1953
Watson & Crick Describe the Structure of DNA
Discovery of the double helix with key contributions from Rosalind Franklin.
Significance: Foundation of molecular biology and genetics.
1969
Apollo 11 Moon Landing
Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon.
Significance: Major milestones in space exploration and human achievement.
2000s ‚Äì 21st Century Science & Technology
2001
Human Genome Project Draft Completed
First map of all human genes.
Significance: Revolutionized medicine, genetics, and biotechnology.
2012
Discovery of the Higgs Boson
Confirmed the existence of the Higgs field, essential for understanding mass.
Significance: Major victory for particle physics and the Standard Model.
