Prehistoric Era (before 1500 CE)

~2.5 million years ago
First Stone Tools
Early humans (Homo habilis) created simple stone tools for cutting and hunting.
Marked the beginning of technology and gave humans an evolutionary advantage.
~1 million years ago
Control of Fire
Evidence shows early humans learned to make and control fire.
Provided warmth, protection, cooking, and social gathering, shaping human evolution.
~10,000 BCE
Development of Agriculture
Humans began farming in the Fertile Crescent and other regions.
Led to permanent settlements, population growth, and the rise of civilizations.
Ancient Civilizations
Early Astronomy
Egyptians, Babylonians, and Mayans tracked stars and planets to create calendars.
Allowed for accurate farming cycles, navigation, and early scientific thought.

1500s â€“ The Dawn of Modern Science

1543
Copernicus Publishes On the Revolutions of the Celestial Spheres
Proposed the heliocentric model, placing the Sun at the center of the universe instead of Earth.
Challenged centuries of geocentric belief and reshaped astronomy.
1545
Andreas Vesalius Publishes De Humani Corporis Fabrica
First detailed and accurate study of human anatomy.
Corrected ancient misconceptions and advanced medicine and surgery.
1572
Tycho Brahe Observes a Supernova
Proved the heavens were not unchanging, contradicting Aristotelian views.
Paved the way for modern astronomy and understanding of stellar life cycles.
1590s
Invention of the Compound Microscope
Attributed to Hans & Zacharias Janssen.
Allowed scientists to study microorganisms and cells for the first time.

1609

1620

Introduced the scientific method, emphasizing observation and experimentation.
Laid the foundation for modern scientific inquiry.
1665

Coined the term "cell" while observing cork under a microscope.
Birth of cell theory in biology.
1687
Isaac Newton Publishes Principia Mathematica
Defined laws of motion and universal gravitation.
Revolutionized physics and astronomy.
